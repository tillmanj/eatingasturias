{% newthought 'An *ethnic group* is a category of people' %} that are, within a larger society, set apart from that larger group, and bound to each other more closely, through the common ties of language, culture, sometimes race, and very rarely by nationality.

Fredrik Barth, building on the prior work of Max Weber, held that these ethnic groups were a social construct{% sidenote 'sn01' ' *künstlich* (artificial) in Weber´ s formulation. See his **Economy and Society: An Outline of Interpretive Sociology** [1921] for a complete treatment.' %}, contrary to the then prevailing belief of the time, which held that socio-cultural and behavioral differences between peoples stemmed from inherited traits and tendencies derived from common descent, commonly called "race". Weber in 1921, and following him, Barth in 1969, held that an ethnic group was based in a shared belief in a shared cultural community.{% sidenote 'sn02' '*Gemeinschaft* (community) in Weber´s formuation. See Barth´s **Ethnic groups and boundaries. The social organization of culture difference** [1969] for his theory of ethnicity as "ongoing negotiations of boundaries between groups of people".' %}

## A Language Community
A language, particularly a minority language, develops a group identity among it's users.
